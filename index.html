PATCHWORK

Group projects are a big part of the university experience, but many teams struggle with conflict and miscommunication. Patchwork is a narrative-based card game designed to help students learn more about themselves and their teammates before starting a group project through collaborative decision making and negotiating resources. The game unfolds over six phases, set in a fictional village, where players face scenarios inspired by common group project challenges. At the end of the game, each player receives a work style archetype and a group synergy score, offering insight into how they collaborate as a team.

Our Work-Type Character Archetypes:

The Strategist

You like structure, clarity, and doing things properly. While others jump into ideas quickly, you’re thinking about the details, the plan, and what could go wrong. You make sure the group’s work is organized and high quality.

The Driver

You’re usually the one pushing the group forward when things stall. When everyone is still discussing ideas, you’re already thinking about what needs to get done and how to start. You’re comfortable making quick decisions and stepping up when no one else does.

The Supporter

You’re the person making sure everyone feels heard. You notice when someone is being left out or when tensions start rising, and you help bring the group back together. You value cooperation and keeping things running smoothly.

The Motivator
